  6. (1) (Mark)由题设可知, $X_i(i=1,2,\ldots,n)$相互独立, 且$X_i\sim b(1,p)$, $P\{X_i=x_i\}=p^{x_i}(1-p)^{1-x_i}$, 其中$x_i=0,1$. 于是得到
    $$\begin{aligned} P\{X_1=x_1, X_2=x_2,\ldots, X_n=x_n\}&=\prod_{i=1}^nP\{X_i=x_i\} =\prod_{i=1}^n p^{x_i}(1-p)^{1-x_i}\\ &=p^{\sum_{i=1}^nx_i}(1-p)^{n-\sum_{i=1}^nx_i}. \end{aligned}$$

    (2) (Mark)因为$X_i(i=1,2,\ldots,n)$相互独立, 且$X_i\sim b(1,p)$, 故$\sum\limits_{i=1}^nX_i\sim b(n,p)$, 所以其分布律为
    $$P\left\{\sum_{i=1}^nX_i=k\right\}={n\choose k}p^k(1-p)^{1-k}, \qquad k=0,1,\ldots,n.$$

    (3) 由第四章第2节例6可知
    $$E(\overline{X})=p,\quad D(\overline{X})=\frac{p(1-p)}{n}, \quad E(S^2)=p(1-p).$$
